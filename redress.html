Redress

Historical trauma through interactive narrative.

Role UX/UI Designer
Platform Twine (Interactive Fiction)
Collaboration Prof. Lien Tran, Full Spectrum Features
Type Research Assistantship / Social Impact Game
Historical Narrative Twine Systems RA Research Affective Design

Overview

Redress is an interactive Twine-based narrative game designed to teach high school and college students about Asian American history and civil liberties, particularly surrounding Japanese American incarceration during WWII. The game emphasizes empathy, agency, and historical understanding through player-driven storytelling.

Design Goals

  • Make historical information experiential through choices and consequences
  • Empower players with perspective-taking and critical thinking
  • Avoid trauma portrayal; instead, focus on resilience, context, and redress
  • Deliver educational content subtly through meaningful gameplay mechanics

My Contributions

  • Prototyped a hi-fi wireframe of the game's interface in Twine
  • Designed dynamic UI/UX systems for dialogue box mechanics (floating text, dialogue box switching based on character)
  • Progress indicators and player memory trackers
  • Codex-style Historical Glossary integration
  • Crafted logic flow for player choices using advanced macros
  • Collaborated on narrative tone and branching story design
  • Ensured visual consistency with accessibility (font hierarchy, contrast, flow)

Impact

Redress was part of a larger public history project in partnership with Full Spectrum Features, meant to preserve underrepresented stories and build educational tools that stick emotionally and cognitively. This work directly contributed to an interactive teaching tool that could enter classrooms across the country.
