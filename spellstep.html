Overview

Neurodivergent students and professionals often struggle with task initiation due to overwhelm, perfection paralysis, and emotional burnout. SpellStep reimagines productivity through story-first design, transforming everyday tasks into a gentle narrative journey supported by emotional cues, character arcs, and kindness-based motivation.

Your tasks aren't chores — they're quests. Your progress isn't a checklist — it's a world that responds to you. The quest begins with you.

UX Challenges

ND users often struggle with traditional to-do apps due to:

Overwhelming list layouts
Punitive streak loss
Lack of emotional resonance
Rigid structure that doesn't adapt to energy levels

SpellStep's UI needed to be: gentle, expressive, adaptable, and emotionally supportive without infantilizing users.

Research Foundation

  • Built on Self-Determination Theory (SDT)
  • Supports autonomy, competence, and relatedness
  • Designed around neurodivergent emotional load, not neurotypical assumptions
  • Prioritizes pressure-free engagement

Core Mechanics

  • Break tasks into user-defined "spell steps" (micro-tasks)
  • Progress map that visualizes emotional & narrative advancement
  • Companion-driven feedback replacing punitive systems
  • Dynamic streaks: not punishment-based, but story-based

Design Solutions

1. Kindness-Based Motivation System

Companion gives affirmation, not pressure. Visual cues like soft glows + "world reaction" instead of red warnings. Progress reinforces competence without guilt.

2. Narrative Progress Map

Each completed task unlocks a piece of the story world. Reflects emotional journey, not just productivity. Encourages return through curiosity rather than obligation.

3. Flexible Task-Chaining

Users chunk tasks into manageable steps. System adapts to low-energy or high-energy states. Removes shame around task size or pace.

4. Visual Language

Soft, painterly aesthetic inspired by cozy fantasy. Gentle color gradients to reduce cognitive load. Animation rules: slow, calming, low-noise.

Results

  • Reduces overwhelm by breaking down cognitive load
  • Converts emotional friction into motivation loops
  • Supports ND users without guilt-based mechanics
  • Creates narrative attachment → higher voluntary engagement
