Narrative Design Case Study

Space Odyssey

Visual novel narrative design & UX writing for an interactive refugee story.

Role Lead Researcher · Narrative Designer · UX/UI Designer
Platform Figma
Timeline Semester-long academic project
Narrative Design UX Writing Branching Narrative Character Design Playtesting

Overview

Space Odyssey is an interactive visual novel exploring identity, displacement, and survival through a branching sci-fi narrative. Players navigate a war-torn galaxy as a customizable protagonist making choices that shape relationships, moral standing, and story outcomes across three chapters.

The Challenge

Create a branching narrative system where player choices feel meaningful, character voice remains consistent across divergent paths, and moral complexity drives emotional engagement without relying on binary good/evil mechanics.

Initial playtesting revealed critical issues:

  • Story lacked character depth and worldbuilding
  • Choices felt flat and emotionally unimpactful
  • Players could "call the bluff" — recognizing that narrative stakes weren't real

Research & Approach

I grounded the narrative redesign in the Narrative Taxonomy of Choices, focusing on creating moral tension even when choices were structurally constrained. The goal was to make "fake" choices feel emotionally real by embedding them in character relationships and worldbuilding consequences.

Key Design Principle: Illusion of agency matters as much as actual branching. If players believe their choices reflect their values, the narrative succeeds — even if paths converge later.

Narrative Iteration Process

First Iteration

  • Initial story lacked character depth and worldbuilding
  • Choices felt flat — emotionally unimpactful and low-stakes
  • Playtest feedback: narrative didn't resonate; players weren't invested

Second Iteration

  • Applied Narrative Taxonomy of Choices to restructure player decisions
  • Planted moral tension in every choice point
  • Result: Playtesters immediately identified weak narrative illusion — this insight drove the final rewrite

Third Iteration

  • Introduced sub-narratives branching off each major decision
  • Side threads funneled back into the main plot, preserving narrative cohesion
  • Strengthened illusion of agency while keeping emotional arcs tight and intentional

Character Design & Voice

I developed three main characters with distinct voices, backstories, and emotional arcs:

Zaira Morvayne

Age 39 · Doctor · Herbalist. Intellectual, sharp, emotionally guarded. Witty and prone to pessimism. The family's anchor — stubborn, brilliant, disorganized.

Sorin Morvayne

Age 44 · Skymarshal. Direct, commanding, duty-driven. Carries the weight of every soldier he's lost. Buries his soft heart under armor and discipline.

Orius Morvayne

Age 17. Uses humor to deflect pain, seeks his father's approval but never gets it. Soft heart hidden under sarcasm — the emotional core of the story.

Narrative Structure

I designed a three-chapter branching system:

  • Chapter 1 — The War: Establishes core conflict (war, identity erasure, survival). Players choose to leave or stay after an attack, with sub-branches for each path.
  • Chapter 2 — New World: Introduces moral ambiguity — refugee status, illegal immigration, seeking asylum. Players navigate bureaucratic and human obstacles.
  • Chapter 3 — Finding Hope: Escalates consequences based on prior choices. Players find community, isolate, or forge new connections.

Playtesting & Results

After Iteration 2

  • Playtesters immediately identified weak points in narrative illusion
  • Feedback shaped the decision to introduce sub-narratives rather than purely linear branching

After Iteration 3

  • Players reported stronger emotional investment in character relationships
  • Moral tension made choices feel consequential even when mechanical outcomes were limited

Key Learnings

  • Moral tension > mechanical branching — Players care more about choices that reflect their values than choices that change the plot
  • Voice consistency across branches is critical — Character dialogue must feel authentic whether the player is aggressive, diplomatic, or avoidant
  • Iteration based on real feedback > theory — The Narrative Taxonomy of Choices provided structure, but playtester reactions revealed what actually worked emotionally
  • Sub-narratives preserve agency without sprawl — Branching paths can funnel back to a main story without feeling "fake" if emotional consequences persist
